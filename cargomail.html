Cargomail, a Revised Email System

Email is the most pervasive form of business information exchange. It is used as an interpersonal communication tool and often as a default choice to send files. Despite the importance of the email system, the whole ecosystem relies on 50-year-old architecture and protocol design.


Cargomail uses a unique way of organizing data communication by introducing a new type of mailbox called a resource mailbox. This is achieved by separating the identifier and the locator of the address/mailbox pair. With this architecture, digital resources such as messages, documents, images, videos, and audio can be exchanged efficiently. Cargomail technology offers better protection against unwanted emails and helps to keep your emails safe and private. Many people can benefit from using this technology. For instance, doctors can send X-rays, CT scans, or MRI scans to other doctors for advice.


In the long run, the goal is to use this technology to enable the construction of a global digital resource infrastructure to help exchange coherent and trustworthy information on the Internet. Many other advances in using this new architecture are described in the Cargomail white paper. The paper is intended to convey both the "big picture" and some technical details. I recommend that you read the first page of the white paper, then Chapter V "Advantages Compared to the Current Email System," and finally, Chapter VIII "Use Cases" to grasp the "big picture" before diving into the details.
