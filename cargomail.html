Cargomail, a revised email system

Email is the most pervasive form of business information exchange. It is used as an interpersonal communication tool and often as a default choice to send files. Despite the importance of the email system, the whole ecosystem relies on 50-year-old architecture and protocol design.


Cargomail uses an architecture that separates the identifier and the locator of the address/mailbox pair to introduce a new type of mailbox—a resource mailbox. This approach offers a new way of organizing data transmission. It enables the exchange of digital resources (assets), e.g., messages, documents, images, audio, and videos. Many other advances in using this new architecture are described in the Cargomail white paper. The paper is intended to convey both the "big picture" and some technical details. I recommend you read the paper's first page and Section V to understand the "big picture" before delving into the details.
