Identity-Based Network

Identity-Based Network (IBN) Architecture

The Internet of Everything is a complex interconnection of people, processes, software and hardware with unique identifiers and the ability to exchange data. The networking industry has to agree on how to assemble the trusted, secure infrastructure for connecting those various entities.

The IBN network architecture that is built on top of the WebRTC network can be thought of as a network of trust identities. The WebRTC peer-to-peer technology in combination with federated Identity and Relationship Management creates a composite IBN architecture that meets the needs of the Internet of Everything ecosystem.

This trust-to-trust communication can be divided into five phases.

IBN Architecture - Phase I-II.
get authorization, set up a relationship

IBN Architecture - Phase III-IV.
get authorization, session establishment

IBN Architecture - Phase V.
exchange data

This architecture does not add an extra layer to the network, it is a pure combination of existing systems and technologies and thus the overhead added by IBP to the network is negligible.
