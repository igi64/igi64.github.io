Global Reference Identity Protocol (GRIP)

Suppose you have received a confirmation email during the sign-up or password reset process. In that case, you're aware that the email infrastructure forms the backbone of the users' global digital identity. Just as messages are sent and forwarded in an email system, GRIP "sends" and "forwards" security tokens.


The GRIP mechanism relies on the existing global Domain Name System (DNS) and Public Key Infrastructure (PKI). The goal is to build an infrastructure that enables identity propagation between untrusted systems and applications across the Internet.


The white paper for GRIP is currently unavailable, but it is expected to be released in the second half of 2023. In the meantime, you can still browse the GRIP repository.
