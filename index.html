Identity-Based Privacy

I am a software engineer with over twenty years accumulated experience in the software architecture, development and product design. I'd like to transform my knowledge to a useful product or service that customers wish to use and buy. I have designed and developed a new decentralized Identity-Based Privacy (IBP) protection system and I am currently working on practical business application to protect sensitive information.

IBP system is a lightweight alternative to PKI/PGP/IBE systems. In combination with OAuth or Mozilla Persona authentication, offers a new way to protect your data in the Cloud without the need to exchange certificates or memorize passwords.

IBP at its heart relies on Identity-Based-One-Time-Key Generator running as a trusted keychain server.

    There are three main types of keychain servers:
  • 1. Any corporate server can run as a trusted keychain server.
  • 2. You can turn your own smartphone, tablet or home computer into your private keychain server.
  • 3. You can use some of the fallback keychain servers that I hope will be available in the near future.

IBP uses client-side zero-knowledge encryption. All users' data are encrypted on the client side and never touch servers in a plain form. Cloud data storage provider has zero knowledge of the encryption keys and key generator server has zero knowledge of users' data.

Main feature: Shared privacy!
As in real life your shared privacy with your family, friends or colleagues is determined by their names, in IBP system your shared privacy is determined by their email addresses.
