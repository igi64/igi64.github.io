Identity-Based Privacy

TLTR
I am a software engineer with over twenty years accumulated experience in the software architecture, development and product design. I'd like to transform my knowledge to a useful product or service that customers wish to use and buy. I have designed and developed a new decentralized Identity-Based Privacy (IBP) protection system and I am currently working on practical business application to protect sensitive information.

If you read my blog post from 26th May, 2013 you can find that Cloud Computing and Information Privacy is a real issue. Information privacy is defined as individual information governance and if we borrow from Separation of powers model in Ancient Greece and Rome, the outcome is:

Separation of powers in Cloud Computing

It is a model for the governance of a data in Cloud Computing divided into three branches each with separate and independent powers and areas of responsibility so that no branch has more power than the other branches. The typical division of branches is into an identity and access management, an encryption key management, and an application with data storage. In the center of these branches is user/customer. It is recommended that at least one branch is under full user control, running on-premises. An encryption key management installed and running on computers on the premises is highlighted as the best practice Cloud Computing model.

IBP system is divided into:

  • Identity management and access control based on OpenID Connect.
  • Encryption key management based on Identity-Based Encryption Key Generator.
  • Data storage based on elFinder web file manager with WebDAV endpoint.

IBP system is a lightweight alternative to PKI/PGP/IBE systems. In combination with OpenID Connect authentication and authorization, offers a new way to protect your data in the Cloud without the need to exchange certificates or memorize passwords.

IBP at its heart relies on Identity-Based Encryption Key Generator running as a trusted keyring server and identity management/access control based on OAuth 2.0 or OpenID Connect.

The three types of keyring servers:

  • Any corporate server can run as a trusted keyring server.
  • You can turn your own smartphone, tablet or home computer into your private keyring server.
  • You can use some of the fallback keyring servers that I hope will be available in the near future.

IBP uses client-side zero-knowledge encryption. All users' data are encrypted on the client side and never touch servers in a plain form. Cloud data storage provider has zero knowledge of the encryption keys and key generator server has zero knowledge of users' data.

Main feature: Shared privacy!
As in real life your shared privacy with your family, friends or colleagues is determined by their names, in IBP system your shared privacy is determined by their email addresses.
